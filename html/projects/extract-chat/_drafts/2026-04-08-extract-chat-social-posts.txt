LINKEDIN
==================================================
`extract-chat` is a chat log extraction tool for ChatGPT JSON logs.

It started from a simple frustration: exporting a ChatGPT conversation gives you the data, but not something you'd actually want to read, reuse, archive, or hand to someone else. Much less feed into a new ChatGPT session for context continuity.

`extract-chat` takes exported ChatGPT or OpenAI conversation JSON and turns it into readable Markdown or HTML while keeping the structure that still matters for real downstream work.

It also pairs nicely with LogGPT, a Safari extension available on the App Store:
https://apps.apple.com/us/app/loggpt/id6743342693?mt=12

LogGPT saves the conversation locally, and `extract-chat` turns that export into something you can actually work with - including splitting Markdown into smaller chunks for context continuity with overlap.

If you care about preserving useful conversations instead of leaving them trapped in a browser tab or a raw JSON file, this may be useful.

Write-up:
https://unixwzrd.ai/projects/extract-chat/2026/03/28/extract-chat-introduction/

If you're trying to turn AI output into something usable and it's not quite working the way you expected, I can help with that. Feel free to reach out.

Title:
Introducing extract-chat

Subtitle:
Turning exported ChatGPT conversations into readable Markdown or HTML instead of leaving them stranded in raw JSON.

Body:
I published a write-up on `extract-chat`, a tool I built for a problem that felt increasingly common: you can export a conversation from ChatGPT, but the JSON you get back is not something most people want to read or actually work with.

`extract-chat` converts that exported conversation into readable Markdown or HTML while preserving the parts of the structure that still matter. That makes it more useful for archiving, publishing, reviewing, chunking, and feeding into later workflows.

It also complements LogGPT, which is available on the App Store as a Safari extension. LogGPT helps with saving the conversation locally, and `extract-chat` makes that exported data readable and reusable.

If you are already using LogGPT, this is the natural next step. If you are not, the core idea still stands: conversation history should be portable and usable, not locked into a browser session or buried in a raw export file.
